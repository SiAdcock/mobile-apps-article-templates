19:01

Simon will be here shortly. In the meantime, here’s an excerpt from David Hytner’s preview:

It is important to stress that Tim Sherwood was joking. The subject was Roberto Soldado and the Tottenham Hotspur manager was asked whether he had tried any old tricks in an attempt to rebuild the striker’s confidence – even getting him to bang balls into empty nets in training. “What about if he misses?” Sherwood shot back.

The way things are going for Soldado, the possibility cannot be discounted. The £26m signing from Valencia missed an open goal in last Thursday’s Europa League last-32 first-leg at Dnipro and on Sunday he fired over from an extremely presentable opportunity at Norwich City in the Premier League. Tottenham lost both matches 1-0.

Soldado’s first season in English football has been an ordeal, in which the errors have tended to stick more readily in the memory. The 28-year-old is actually Tottenham’s joint-top scorer with 10 goals yet there are caveats – five of them have come in the Europa League and four of his five in the league have been penalties.

It said much, though, about Sherwood’s breezy and ballsy man-management style that he was prepared to poke a little fun at Soldado who, he said, would start in the return tie against Dnipro at White Hart Lane on Thursday night.

Sherwood added that he had offered Soldado reassurance since the Norwich game. He told the striker the tide would turn and he simply needed any sort of goal, even one that deflected in off his backside to make it happen. He also pointed out that even the very best players endure barren spells; Liverpool’s Luis Suárez, for example, is on a run of one goal in eight starts. “No one is pointing any fingers as Liverpool are winning games,” Sherwood said.

Hello!

A hamstring injury has sadly prevented Jermain Defoe from pulling on his Spurs shirt one last time and adding to his 143 Tottenham goals before he sails off into don’t-call-it-retirement in Toronto, but he will nevertheless stride onto the pitch and luxuriate in the fans’ adoration at half-time. And perhaps, Tottenham’s form being not entirely convincing and Dnipro having a 1-0 first-leg advantage to desperately cling onto, this will prove our evening’s highlight.

And thus my pre-match question: what is the finest and/or weirdest half-time entertainment you have ever experienced at a football match? I’ve had dog shows, I’ve been sung at by Chas & Dave and Right Said Fred (not at the same time) and various talent-show wannabes, I’ve failed to make any sense at all of an on-pitch interview with Ozzy Osbourne, I’ve seen marching bands and cheerleaders’ dance routines and Sylvester Stallone waving an Everton flag and two mascots actually getting married (in character, rather than real life. Anyway, it didn’t last, and she’s now moved out). Before tonight’s game, though, you can enjoy the musical treat that once enlivened the middle of a match between Watford and Preston.

And here’s the Press Association take on those line-ups:

Midfielder Sandro was handed his first appearance under Tim Sherwood for Tottenham as they looked to overturn a 1-0 deficit in the second leg of their Europa League last-32 meeting with Dnipro. The Brazil international has been missing with a calf problem since December but returned as one of five changes from Sunday’s loss at Norwich.

Zeki Fryers came in for the injured Danny Rose while Christian Eriksen, Andros Townsend and Roberto Soldado all started at White Hart Lane. Dnipro, managed by former Spurs boss Juande Ramos, named an unchanged side from their first-leg victory in Ukraine a week ago.

19:41

The interest tonight won’t be confined to half-time – there’s also some guaranteed pre-match action – here are the key sections of a press release I was sent about it:

Fans of Dnipro will pay tribute to the people killed during recent protests in Ukraine before their Europa League game against Tottenham at White Hart Lane this Thursday.

Supporters from Dnipropetrovsk will join members of the London Ukrainian diaspora in a march to remember those killed during the revolution. The group will march to White Hart Lane with flags, placards, candles and black ribbons.

The first protester to be killed was 20-year-old Serhiy Nihoyan from the Dnipropetrovsk region. Both Dnipro and Tottenham players wore black armbands in honour of the dead during the first leg, and Dnipro supporters groups have been instrumental in organising anti-government protests in their city.

Dnipropetrovsk was also the scene of a remarkable event when hundreds of locals put their own lives at risk by lying on railway tracks in to stop trains full of armed paramilitary police from leaving for Kyiv to join in the violence against protesters.
