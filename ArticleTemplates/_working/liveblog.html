The mystery of the Alex Turner mystery

Last week Alex Turner won a Brit award. Good on the lad. Then he gave a victor’s speech which was impenetrable and a bit rude.

Now, to make things even more weirderer, he’s said that his speech, which looked pretty deliberately delivered in the eyes of this blogger, was all a result of nerves.

In public I’m a quiet guy, so doing anything in front of lots of people always makes me nervous. I’m known for my music, not how well I deliver a speech.

He gave that quote to the Daily Mail, which may make it more or less likely to be honest. But if it were a result of nerves then those flicks of the head and arms, rather than trying to be all Elvisy as some assumed, must have been a tic.

PS That whole bit about the ‘cyclical nature of the universe’ is all a bit True Detective (slight spoiler upcoming)

Rejoice, Heroes is to return!

Remember that programme about ordinary people discovering they had extraordinary powers? No, not Britain’s Got Talent, but Heroes, the US drama that started out alright but quickly got really bad and then just got axed.

Anyway, it’s back for a new 13 episode series or, as the producers are calling it, an ‘event miniseries’. This title has the double advantage of both making the thing sound more dramatic and saving the producers from embarrassment if they have to can it again.

If that wasn’t exciting enough, check out this teaser trailer which basically is nothing more than a bit of noise and a new word

As you would expect... Miley Cyrus performs Yoshimi with Flaming Lips

This happened on Saturday night in Los Angeles. Miley Cyrus, wearing a shirt with her own face on it, proclaims her love for the Flaming Lips and invites Wayne Coyne and Stephen Drozd on stage to perform ‘Yoshimi Battles the Pink Robots’ with her.

It’s an unusual, some might say utterly incompatible collaboration, but it exists. And who knows she could even, as she insists at the beginning of the vid, be an actual fan.

Worth watching just for Miley’s rap hand gestures as she sings about the tragic plight of a future Japanese girl doomed to combat killer robots. That and the complete lack of comprehension as to what is going on on the part of her armies of fans.

Questlove is dead!

It’s with great sadness and no small amount of shock that we bring you the news this morning that beloved Roots drummer, arch-tweeter and man with difficult name to spell ?uestlove has been found, shot dead, by Ice T.

Oh, hang on, we’re getting word in our ear that he’s not dead, just playing a corpse in Ice T’s super-gory cop procedural Law and Order: SVU. So as you were then.
