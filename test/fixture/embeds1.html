James Murphy's second service

IBM and James Murphy have teamed up for an algorithm-led musical project based on data collected from US Open tennis matches. Despite how boring that sounds they’re actually pretty good. Here’s Murphy’s description of match 104:

When this match began, it could have been either player’s game. And like the match that inspired it, this track opens with beats that are balanced–intense but equal, just like the players–with no instrument clearly taking the lead. The music pulses steadily until the last half of the track, when the instruments start to break form as one player falls behind, and the other takes the lead. The track ends with a soft, high-pitched whistle that ushers the defeated player off the court.

Bearing in mind that some matches ran to over four hours, most of the tracks are cut down significantly (on average they clock in around the 30 minute mark). Some are barely more than some weird pong style bleeps but left for long enough they become lush synth journeys, presumably those are the bits where the players rub their faces with towels and swear at the floor.

The cynics among you may think is this a ploy to try and take some column inches from another major tech company - and you’d probably be right.

Serena Williams also teamed up with a musical brand, as she took part in Beats by Dre’s new wireless headphones advert. In it she puts on some music, does a ridiculously hard looking workout and then carries on as usual.

All this of course follows the Drake/Nick Kyrgios beef which started after the Aussie player said he’d played poorly after listening to Drizzy’s tracks. Drake fired back calling him ‘Nick whatever-his-name-is’ before Kyrgios issued a sort of apology via Twitter.

PC Music in LA

We’ve got our own thinkpiece on the rise of PC Music in this weekend’s Guide. Here’s a little snippet for those who aren’t aware of what they’re all about:

AG Cook’s PC Music takes its cues from the Japanese concept of kawaii (cuteness) with a musical pallette encompassing the huge synth blasts favoured by Eurodance chart-botherers such as Cascada, grime’s sub-bass, and happy hardcore’s high-pitched vocal range, matched with promo pics that make its artists look like X Factor contestants from 2054. Their artist names are things like Hannah Diamonds, Princess Bambi and the Lipgloss Twins; their aesthetic, somewhere between an edition of Just 17 and a shopping centre in Shibuya.

Check it out on Saturday, it’s a great read (I wrote it), but to get a better understanding it’s best to actually listen for yourself.

Some of the crew were over in LA with Boiler Room and their sets were (predictably) not the usual foot-shuffling fare, with super slow-mo, PAs and general madness. Both SOPHIE’s and new artist QT’s sets are below as well as label honcho AG Cook’s remix of “indie R&B” pin up How To Dress Well.

Afternoon Tunes: Buke and Gase, Vince Staples, Gengahr

Gwilym here, batting gamely on through the afternoon session of the Guide Daily. CUE THE MUSIC:

Buke And Gase - Seam Esteem

Buke And Gase are a New York-based two-piece who do twisty, layered indie in the manner of St Vincent or Juana Molina. Their USP is that they make their own instruments (a ‘buke’ is a modified baritone-ukelele, apparently, while a ‘gase’ is - yep - a cross between a guitar and a bass). You might assume then that Seam Esteem resembles on of Mulligan and O’Hare’s experimental works, but fear not, it’s actually a winding and pleasingly creepy piece of art-rock.

Vince Staples - Hands Up

Like Lauryn Hill’s Black Rage, Hands Up is a track that was written before the shooting of Michael Brown - Staples has explicitly stated that the song is not about Ferguson - but which captures the discontent felt in its wake quite aptly. It’s easy to imagine this becoming something of an anthem in the coming weeks and months. Further evidence that Staples is, as Lanre wrote recently, “one of the most impressive young MCs to emerge in the last couple of years.”

Gengahr - Powder

I missed this London four-piece at Glasto this year (their BBC Introducing set probably clashed with my weekend-long quest to find the perfect Burrito), but Powder has made me wish I hadn’t. Woozy Unknown Mortal Orchestra falsetto over the top of Coxon-esque guitar squall? Yes please.

BONUS!!! The Replacements - Alex Chilton (live on Jimmy Fallon)

The re-formed Replacements performed Alex Chilton, their 1987 paean to the Big Star lead singer, on Jimmy Fallon last night. It was predictably ace, and has prompted me to re-read Our Band Could Be Your Life for the 28,000th time.

DAFT REALITY SHOW ALERT! DAFT REALITY SHOW ALERT! DAFT REALITY SHOW ALERT!

Titans of trash TV Channel 5 and MTV (now siblings as a result of Viacom’s takeover of 5) have just announced a new joint venture called 10,000 BC, which will see a group of preened and primped 21st century types cast back in time (read: plonked into a forest) to live in a Stone Age community. Here’s a bit of blurb:

This programme is about exchanging modern life for a prehistoric one and to take stock of how our lives have changed. Has it all been for the better? Either way, it’ll be a highly emotionally charged roller coaster ride….tears, tantrums, laughter and joy, every day, 24/7. These men and women will be going back to basics – big time. They’ll be hunting animals, foraging for food, subsisting on whatever they can scour from their environment. They’ll be cold, hungry, scared and pushed to the limits of endurance. They’ll have no one but themselves and each other to help get them through.
