US aid worker held hostage by Isis reportedly killed in coalition air strike

American female hostage has been killed in a US-backed air strike, supporters of Islamic State have claimed on social media

Shiv Malik and Mona Mahmood in London

Published: 17:25 GMT Friday, 06 February 2015

An American female hostage has been killed in a US backed air strike, supporters of Islamic State have claimed on social media.

Several Twitter accounts supportive of the militant group have distributed a short communique claiming a bombing raid by Jordanian planes on the Syrian City of Raqqa early Friday had partly destroyed a building in which the woman was being held. The group offered no evidence to support the claim, which could not be independently verified.

State department Deputy Spokesperson Marie Harf said she could not confirm the report, but confirmed that “a number of Americans” were being held by the Islamic State.

The communique which also claimed to carry the full name and US contact details of the hostage, who the Guardian is not naming, said that no one except the woman had been killed in the strike.

“The criminal crusader Coalition planes targeted sites outside Raqqa city today at noon time while people were performing Friday prayers,” the message read.

“The raids continued on the same site for more than an hour. God has disappointed their endeavours and foiled their plot by not hitting any jihadi man thank God.”

The communique went on to say the hostage had died in the strike.

Other Isis supporters tweeted several pictures of a bombed-out multi story block claiming this was the building that had been targeted by Jordanian planes.

A US aid worker is known to have been held hostage since travelling with her Syrian boyfriend to the war torn country in August.
